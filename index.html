A Brief Background

Scott Harper founded Sunday Night in 2001 after landing his first independent “Cake Night,” a POV behind-the-scenes look at addiction
recovery. New projects include “Clara's Big Ride,”. Viewed by millions, it features Olympian Clara Hughes and looks at the issue of stigma
and mental illness. Scott wrote, produced and directed the award-winning “Lost Adventures of Childhood,” an exploration of the vanishing phenomenon of unsupervised play among kids. The film has sold in 28 countries and counting. Like a real life version of The Office, his
CTV documentary “Sunday Night” took an ironic look at trials and tribulations inside a temp agency. Up next: “You're Soaking In It,” at take
on the disrupted 21st century ad agency. Filming this summer, it will air on CBC TV.
